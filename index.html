Timofey Golubev's website

Welcome to my website! I am a PhD student in Computational Condensed Matter Physics at Michigan State University working on semiconductor device modeling. Here you can find out more about me and my research interests.

Current Research:

Optoelectronic device modeling

I am currently working on modeling of optoelectronic devices such as bilayer organic solar cells. My research is focused on the computational methods for such modeling.

Read More

Density functional theory (DFT)

I am working on DFT using Vienna ab-initio Simulation Package (VASP) with a goal of studying the surface science related to molecular beam epitaxy growth of transition metal dichalcogenides (see experimental work below).

Past Computational Research:

Structural control of light

As an undergraduate at Missouri S&T, I worked on computational light propagation studies such as the diffusion of light through waveguides and optical properties of a two dimensional aperiodic array of microcavities.

Read More

Molecular dynamics

I have written some molecular dynamics code for melting of argon.

Read More

Past Experimental Research:

I have worked on experimental condensed matter projects including perovskite solar cells, nanowire exfoliation, and molecular beam epitaxy (MBE) and scanning tunneling microscopy (STM) characterization of 2D transition metal dichalcogenides.

Molecular beam epitaxy

I worked on molecular beam epitaxy growth of WSe2 and SnSe2 monolayers.

Read More

Mechanical and Liquid Exfoliation

I have worked on mechanical (with use of scotch tape) and liquid (via sonication) exfoliation of transition metal nanowires and also verfied the techniques on MoS2 and Bi2Se3.

Read More

Layers for perovskite solar cells

During my first summer of experimental work, I experimented with techniques of spin coating to achieve higher quality layers for thin-film perovskite solar cells.
